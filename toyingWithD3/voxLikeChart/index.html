Yield Curves

A chart, mainly used by economic experts and investors, called the "yield curve" may be able to predict any recessions. But, what does it actually show? I visualized the Euro-Area yield curves for the past 16 years, for every month, so we are able to perceive what it actually means when the curve changes.

When a yield curve is inverted, it is almost always never a good sign. An inverted yield curve means, that the demand for long-term bonds increased significantly, and the demand for short-term (2-3-year) plummeted.

If that's the case it might be a sign of recession, because if enough investors think that investing in short term returns is not a good idea, an economic downturn is inevitable. If a yield curve is inverted, investors think that an economic downturn is incoming, that is what happened in early '06, and that is what's happening right now, due to COVID-19.

So, is a recession coming in '20? Nobody knows, because financial experts with the proper means, can evade it. Anyway, yield curves are a good way to predict recessions, and that's why some experts freak out when inversion happens.
